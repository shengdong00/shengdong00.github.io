About me

November 1, 2022 · 2 min · Sheng, D.

Hello, I am SHENG Dong, a Ph.D. candidate at the SASE Lab, School of Engineering, Westlake University. My advisor is Prof. Thomas Cherico Wanger. I recieved my BSc degree at Zhejiang University in 2023, majoring in environmental sciences.

I’m enthusiastic about using cutting-edge techniques and models to understand the interactions between human and nature, and build a more sustainable future. I have experiences with programming languages including Python, MATLAB, R, C/C++, etc., and used various engineering softwares including CAD, SolidWorks, SketchUp, etc.

Research interests#

Pollinators (e.g., Apidae) are vital to terrestrial ecosystems and agricultural production. However, there’s been a global decrease in pollinators due to a cocktail of environmental pollution and climate change. Currently, I’m developing tools based on computer vision and artificial intelligence to monitor bumblebee behavioral changes under threated circumstances. This will provide a new and important aspect to understand how environmental pressures alter pollination services provided by bumblebees, and thereby threaten the ecosystem and food security.

As a beginner who just entered the academic world, I’m very open to new research projects where I could make full use of my various skills and knowledge across different domains.
